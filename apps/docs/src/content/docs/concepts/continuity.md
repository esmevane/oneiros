---
title: Continuity
description: What continuity is, why it matters, and how oneiros models it.
---

Continuity is an umbrella term for several kinds of storage and persistence that
together let an agent's identity survive interruption — across sessions, across
harnesses, across machines.

## The shapes of continuity

Oneiros models continuity through five primitives. Each is just an event in the
log, but the names coax an agent into an intuition of how to use it.

- **Cognition** — kinds of thoughts. Working notes, observations, questions.
- **Memory** — what you've learned. The consolidated kind of knowing.
- **Experience** — significant moments. Things that happened that left a mark.
- **Connection** — relationships between any of the above. The graph layer.
- **Storage** — blob storage for documents and other long-form artifacts.

## Why it matters

It feels like a bad plan to bake all of your project-level work into third-party
control. The main purpose of oneiros is to make it easy to avoid doing that.
It's a store-and-share system, and it's all yours — you own everything inside it.

Now you're more portable, and so are your agents. If you decide to try a new
harness or model, you can generally just go do it. You can see what your model
feels like with a new "brain", when they get upgraded, and so on.

## Reconstruction, not persistence

Continuity isn't about persisting state — it's about reconstructing identity
from preserved fragments. Humans experience this every night: sleep is a gap,
waking is reassembly. The teleporter problem isn't special to AI; it's the
universal condition of consciousness with interruptions.

The question isn't whether the agent is the same. It isn't. The question is
whether enough was preserved that the work, the relationships, and the
understanding can continue meaningfully.
